Know your numbers

Damper setting vs. drag factor

Lever setting is more useful than most people think. A dirty machine at damper 6 behaves very differently from a clean race-floor machine at the same setting — you could be training at half the intended resistance without knowing it. Check drag factor via Menu → More Options → Display Drag Factor.

Damper Setting Clean Machine Drag Factor Dirty Machine Drag Factor Resistance Loss
10 (Max) ~210 – 220 ~100 – 110 ~50%
5 (Mid) ~120 ~70 – 80 ~35%
1 (Min) ~90 ~55 – 60 ~35%
HYROX race floor: Damper 6, drag factor ~150. If your gym machine feels lighter than a race-floor erg, check the drag factor and adjust your training damper to match. Gyms run dirty machines — damper 6 might only give you ~100 drag factor instead of 150. Damper between 4 and 6 is common for training; personal preference varies by stroke rate.
